I live in Stockholm but grew up in Mellerud, a small municipality located in western Sweden. I moved to Stockholm in August 2016 to study mathematical statistics and I am currently on my final year of the master programme at Stockholm University.

Working experiences

During my studies I have had multiple part time jobs. In January 2017 i started working at Com Hem, a television and internet provider, as a network documenter and later network planner and I there until August 2019. My task was mainly to map the connections between routers and switches in the network.

In September 2018 I became a teaching assistant at the math department at Stockholm University and have mainly been teching the course Statistical Data Processing, a course in data science which includes data wrangling, data cleaning and visualization of data in R, and introduction to SQL. This fall will be the third year in a row I am teaching in this course. Outside of this course I have also worked with the courses Probability Theory II, Mathematics I, Challenging Mathematics and Preparing Mathematics.

I also began working with IT at the department in January 2019 which includes maintaining our Moodle course pages, WebWork (online homework system for math and sciences courses) and MySQL databases.

Studies

As prevously written, I am currently on my fifth and final year of the master programme in mathematical statistics. My education consists of both theoretical and applied courses which includes subjects like probability theory, Maximum-Likelihood theory, regression, machine learning, deep learning, computer science, finance mathematics, insurance mathematics, random graphs and epidemics to name some. As my bachelor thesis I wrote about rumour spreading models which, if interested, you can read here.

In both my work and my studies I have done a fair share of coding, mostly in R and Python but I also have touched Java, C#, PHP, Mathematica, Matlab and JavaScript.
